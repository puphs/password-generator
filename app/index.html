Password security advices

Don’t use the same password for different sites. Otherwise, someone who culls your Facebook or Twitter password in a phishing exploit could, for example, access your bank account.

If you don’t want to memorize multiple passwords, consider using a password manager. Password managers will automatically update stored passwords, keep them encrypted, and require multi-factor authentication for access.

Avoid using simple words, phrases, and patterns that are easy to guess. Don't use obvious words and phrases like "password", sequences like 1234 or "abcd", "qwerty".

The more sensitive your information is, the more often you should change your password. Once it is changed, do not use that password again for a very long time.

It isn’t hard to discover on the Internet things like your date of birth, the street where you grew up, or the name of your first dog, so do not use these things as your password.
